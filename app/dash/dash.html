About the Tricktionary

The sport of competitive jump roping is a continually growing international community of some of the world's most talented athletes. This app aims to help anyone hoping to become involved in this phenomenal sport by giving anyone with a phone the power to learn all levels and types of jump rope skills. The Tricktionary is organized by type (Basics, Multiples, Power, Rope Manipulation, and Releases) and by level (1-4). It provides a description, a video, prerequisites, and what to learn next for each trick.

A new and powerful tool included in The Jump Rope Tricktionary is the speed timer. With this tool you can time and click speed and be provided with useful statistics about the event. The app displays score, speed, number of misses, an estimated score assuming no misses occurred, and a graph of the jumpers instantaneous speed throughout the entire event.

Also included in the Android™ app is the Show Writer. This enables coaches and jumpers to quickly write jump rope shows in which each athlete has the maximum amount of break time between each of their routines.

The tricktionary is currently available as an Android™ app and as this webapp, if you know how to develop an iOS® app and want to contribute to the Tricktionary, please email us at develop (at) the-tricktionary (dot) com

Explanation of Levels

A checkmark () beside a trick's level means thatthe level has been verified by a certified judge. A golden checkmark () means that the trick's level has been verified by a federation official.

On some occurrences you might notice levels with a dash between two or more numbers, this means that the trick is actually a composition of multiple tricks, the first digit is the level of the first occuring trick followed by a dash and the level of the second trick, and so on.

A typical example of this is a standard Pushup which in fisac-irsf's rulebook is split into two separate tricks, one where you jump into the pushup, and one when you jump out from it.

To report an error in the data, either press "Report an issue with this trick" on the trick's page, or see the Contact page.
